# Research Consent Form — DukaStock Usability Demonstration Session

Required before the SUS demonstration session (proposal Chapter 1.5 /
Chapter 1.3.1, Research Question 3: tertiary). Budgeted in the proposal's
research budget as "Printing (consent forms, SUS) — Kinyarwanda and
English versions." No consent form existed in this repository before this
one — you cannot run the field session without it.

**Print both language versions.** Read the form aloud to the participant
rather than only handing it over, since literacy level varies — this is
standard practice for field research with informal-sector participants,
not an assumption about any individual participant.

**What this form is, and isn't:** it documents that a Duka operator agreed
to a short product demonstration and usability questionnaire. It is
separate from — and should be stored separately from — any data the app
itself collects (which is hashed at ingestion per Rwanda's Law No.
058/2021, per `app.core.security.hash_phone_number`). The consent form
itself will have the participant's name/signature on it, because that is
the standard accountability mechanism for research ethics documentation;
it does not get entered into the DukaStock database.

### What this study is about

I am a student researcher studying whether a computer system can help
shopkeepers like you predict how much sugar, cooking oil, flour, rice, or
soap you might sell next week, and whether that prediction can reach you
through WhatsApp or through a simple phone menu (USSD) — the kind that
works on any phone, not just a smartphone.

### What I am asking you to do

- Try a short demonstration of the system (about 10-15 minutes), using
  either WhatsApp, the USSD menu, or both, on a phone.
- Afterward, answer 10 short questions about how easy or difficult the
  system was to use. There are no right or wrong answers — I want your
  honest opinion.
- Optionally, answer a few background questions (e.g. what you sell, how
  long you've run your shop) to help describe who took part.

This will take about 20-25 minutes in total.

### Your participation is voluntary

- You do not have to take part, and you can stop at any time, for any
  reason, without any negative consequence to you.
- You can skip any question you don't want to answer.
- Deciding not to participate, or to stop partway through, will not affect
  you or your business in any way.

### Risks and benefits

- There is no more than minimal risk in taking part — this is a product
  demonstration and short questionnaire, not a medical or financial
  intervention. No real money or real sales are affected during the demo.
- There is no guarantee of direct personal benefit. Your feedback may help
  improve tools for shopkeepers like you in the future.
- As a token of appreciation for your time, you will receive 2,000 RWF in
  airtime credit, sent to your phone.

### What happens to your information

- Your usability answers (the 10-question scores) are recorded without
  your name attached — only a participant number (e.g. "P1").
- If you use the actual WhatsApp/USSD system during the demo, any phone
  number involved is converted into an anonymous code before it is stored
  anywhere, and your real phone number is never saved in the system's
  database.
- Your name and signature on this paper form are kept only for research
  record-keeping, stored separately from the demonstration data, and are
  not shared outside the research team and supervisor.
- Results will be reported only in combined/summarized form in the
  student's thesis (e.g. "average usability score across participants"),
  never singling out an individual by name.
